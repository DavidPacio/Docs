18 The blockchain is a single-entry system which keeps record of the event of asset transfer. A “single-entry bookkeeping system in a blockchain” is introduced to replace traditional double-entry system (Greg Simon, 2016), while another group of cryptographers provides discussions about “triple-entry” system (Ian Grigg, 2005). The definition of “triple-entry” accounting system used by blockchain-accounting practitioners is different from Ijiri (1986)’s definition. The blockchain based “triple-entry” system is an enhancement to the traditional double-entry system where all accounting entries are recorded by a third entry into the blockchain. In contrast to traditional accounting that the trading partners independently book debit and credit to their own accounts, blockchain’s shared transaction records link the journal entries of trading parties, which provides additional assurance in auditing
