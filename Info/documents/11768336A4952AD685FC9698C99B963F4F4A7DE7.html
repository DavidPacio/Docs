dDollar virtual denomination
----------------------------
Let's admit that working with 8 decimals (or more) currencies is not easy for most brains wired to use FIAT currencies (e.g. $USD, $CAN, Euro, ...). It's convenient to deal with 2 decimals and be able to assess value in an easy to grasp scale (https:⁄⁄en.wikipedia.org⁄wiki⁄Price).
 
Until now, attempts to solve this dilemma (i.e. volatility⁄value⁄convenience) has failed (https:⁄⁄tokenmonk.com⁄basecoin-money-out-of-thin-air-d895fba562a2).
 
The solution is not to create a new token, I propose to establish a stable universal dDollar (D$) denomination (a virtual denomination if you will) to ease the transition toward the new cryptoeconomy.
 
Let's say that, by consensus, we state that a D$ is equivalent of 0.001 oz of gold as of January 1st 2018. Using this denomination, we can establish a fixed (absolute) system of value (no volatility, no inflation, no deflation). Then, if you can buy a bike for 100 D$ in 2018, chances are that you may buy a similar bike for a similar price in 2025, unless of course that significant technological advances allow for cost reduction over time.
 
Today, FIAT currencies are debt-base, and continually lose value, making it difficult to trace absolute value in time. You think that your USD$ has not lost value because the car industry improves so that you can still buy a decent car with 20,000 USD$ 5 years later. With D$, you would realize that 5 years later, your car cost more than 15% less due to industrial processes optimization and technological progression.
 
With D$, we could then express any business document, portfolio or contract (invoices, salaries, orders, estimations, ...) to reflect a universal system of value that could be converted on the fly in any cryptocurrency (e.g. on the back-end for payment). Imagine exchanges converting your trades in D$ on the fly, so you have a consistent experience of an asset value.
 
No oracles needed, no token created, no inflation, no deflation, 2 decimals system (as we are used to), no regulation challenges, ...

DG: Has anyone heard of have experience of Chile's use of the "Unidad de Fomento" or UF – a stable unit of exchange?
"Unidad de Fomento" - A non-circulating currency in Chile. It is adjusted for inflation relative to the Chilean peso (it was formerly adjusted relative to the Chilean escudo) so that its value remains constant. It was created in 1967 and was originally used to help the Chilean government pay its loans for international development.
Interesting and established concept
