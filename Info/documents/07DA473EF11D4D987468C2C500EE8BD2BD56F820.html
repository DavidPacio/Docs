From DG:
“Liberate, organise, account for, and trade the world's data to make it more useful for all” is about as unconvincing as it’s possible to get.  To me it says “something inexplainable for no one in particular”.  It could be expresses as “a, b, c and d to X for (A to Z)”
 
In this sense it is the opposite of everything that the (very shallow) article David points to advises.  The article confuses Context statement, mission, value proposition
 
The one line Context Statement below consist to two parts: Who and What or as the article states - help X achieve Y.  They are not mission statements and not value propositions.  They just describe the “field of play”.
 
As such they are important but just a foot in the door.  If you can’t describe your chosen “field of play”, any audience will lose interest interested in what you have to say.
 
An inability to succinctly articulate the context of your proposal also makes it impossible to build a compelling case going forward.
 
The starting point should be what skills do the founders have and how will they enable the enterprise to help a specific audience.
 
I believe that Pacio needs to get this agreed as a first step.
 
David
