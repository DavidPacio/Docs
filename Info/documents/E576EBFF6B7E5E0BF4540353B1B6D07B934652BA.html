They will probably continue talking about Holochain for the same reason Guy will continue bombarding David with emails about Tender and EOS: they see flaws in their designs and need either justification or a solution.
 
There are two ultimate arguments to their questions:
(1) Holochain is more flexible - an app can be configured to be as good⁄bad as a cosmos app in terms of security, incl. global ordering, but (2) have far greater scalability at a fraction of cost.
 
We have the answers, no problem. Some lower level implementation details of the Tender design approved in January are being refined now. Upon meeting practical constrains.
 
Blockchain is not dead, by the way. DLT systems just get more complex, evolve. We, for example, employ a mix of personal chains and community blockchains at the moment:
- each user maintains their own transaction chain for scalability and security, those chains secure each other when they intertwine;
- developers package their voting into governance blockchain (parliament sessions) in the governance app;
- the laws they pass (emission⁄absorption⁄currency creation⁄etc) are packaged in a separate blockchain and forwarded to the transfer app. Strict ordering and security is required here, of course.

• Holochain is a data-integrity engine for powering distributed apps without encountering the scalability bottlenecks common to blockchains. It has no built-in currency.
• Holo is a hosting company that provides hosting infrastructure for Holochain apps to be served to regular web browsers. Holo works by tapping into computing power wherever it already is, providing an alternative to centralized hosting services like Amazon, Google, and Microsoft’s Azure. This is analogous to how AirBnB sells more room-nights than the largest hotel chains without ever having built a hotel. Holo does have a native currency, HoloFuel, to account for all the micro-transactions involved in hosting.
